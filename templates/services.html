Teen Therapy

The teenage years can be both exciting and challenging as young people navigate emotional, academic, and social pressures. Teen therapy provides a safe space for adolescents to express themselves, explore their feelings, and develop the skills needed to thrive. At Mental Time, we use evidence-based psychological approaches tailored to meet the unique needs of teenagers, helping them manage challenges like anxiety, self-esteem, peer pressure, and family conflicts.We ensure to include talking, playing, or other activities to help the child express feelings and thoughts.

    WHAT TO EXPECT ON TEEN THERAPY.
  • Initial Session:

    The first session is typically with the teen and parents to understand the family dynamics, challenges, and goals. This is also a chance to explain how therapy works and answer questions.

  • Ongoing Sessions:

    Sessions are usually 45-60 minutes and may be weekly or biweekly. Some sessions may involve parents or caregivers to strengthen family communication.

  • Confidentiality:

    Teens need to feel safe in therapy. While sessions are confidential, therapists will share important insights with parents if the teen's safety is at risk.

  • Progress & Feedback:

    Therapy is a collaborative process, with regular check-ins to discuss progress and adjust goals as needed.
